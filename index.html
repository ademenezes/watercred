Model method

Faithful to the workbook, explicit about its limits.

Each factor receives 0–4 points from the workbook's threshold table. Points are multiplied by fixed weights totaling 100. The index is the weighted total divided by the 400-point maximum, multiplied by 100.

Index=Σ (factor score × weight)÷400 × 100

The labels “Creditworthy”, “Potentially creditworthy”, “Below threshold” and “High concern” are demo conventions because the workbook does not define rating bands or lending cut-offs.

75–100Indicatively creditworthy
60–74Potentially creditworthy
40–59Below threshold
0–39High concern

Provisional rule: 60 is the minimum score indicating potential creditworthiness. At least 70% of model inputs—17 of 23—should also be supported by current evidence before presenting the result as a reliable creditworthiness indication. This is not an agency letter rating.

Which workbook sheet is used?

The primary Basis for index score sheet: 23 indicators and weights totaling 100. The alternate Merging O&M sheet combines three cost ratios and is not used.

What comes from Georgia?

The model uses Geostat's 2025 absolute poverty rate of 7.1%. The evidence explorer reads all 345 validated CSV observations across 2014–2024.

What needs due diligence?

Twenty-two model inputs need Georgia utility evidence: audited accounts, billing and collection ledgers, debt schedules, staffing, production volumes, service coverage, and tariff-to-cost data.

Known workbook ambiguities

The live factor formula produces 49.0 for the workbook example, while a separate static cell reports 48.5. Several bands also meet at the same boundary, the staff table omits 5 staff/1,000 connections, and liquidity is scored with EBITDA-style percentage bands despite being defined as a ratio. The demo uses the reproducible 49.0 result and documents deterministic boundary rules in the source code.
