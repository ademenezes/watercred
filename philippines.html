Rank Utility Creditworthiness score Evidence

A relative, renormalized indication — not the full 23-factor index and not a credit rating. Each utility is scored on whichever of the 23 workbook factors its validated data supports, using the workbook's 0–4 bands; weights are renormalized to the available factors (score = Σ points × weight ÷ Σ 4 × weight × 100). At least four evidenced factors are required to rank. Observations more than five years old are excluded, and derived ratios (EBITDA margin, debt to equity, staff per 1,000 connections) only combine same-year financial statements. Liquidity uses current-ratio bands and negative equity scores 0 on leverage — both demo conventions disclosed here.

Model method

Faithful to the workbook, explicit about its limits.

Each factor receives 0–4 points from the workbook's threshold table. Points are multiplied by fixed weights totaling 100. The index is the weighted total divided by the 400-point maximum, multiplied by 100.

Index=Σ (factor score × weight)÷400 × 100

The labels “Creditworthy”, “Potentially creditworthy”, “Below threshold” and “High concern” are demo conventions because the workbook does not define rating bands or lending cut-offs.

75–100Indicatively creditworthy
60–74Potentially creditworthy
40–59Below threshold
0–39High concern

Provisional rule: 60 is the minimum score indicating potential creditworthiness. At least 70% of model inputs—17 of 23—should also be supported by current evidence before presenting the result as a reliable creditworthiness indication. This is not an agency letter rating.

What comes from the Philippines?

The poverty rate uses PSA's 2023 Full Year Official Poverty Statistics (15.5% of the population). Five simulator inputs are seeded from portfolio medians of the validated dataset. The evidence explorer reads all 1,610 validated observations across 57 water districts, 2009–2024, sourced from COA Annual Audit Reports and LWUA monitoring data.

How is the ranking computed?

Up to nine of the 23 workbook factors can be evidenced per utility: O&M coverage, collection efficiency, non-revenue water, energy and staff cost shares, current liquidity, EBITDA margin (reported, or derived from net income, depreciation, interest and revenue), debt to equity, and staff per 1,000 connections. Each is scored with the workbook's 0–4 bands and weights are renormalized to the factors available. Utilities with fewer than four evidenced factors are listed as unranked.

What needs due diligence?

The remaining model inputs need utility-level evidence: debt schedules and service coverage for DSCR, cash reserves, grant dependency, debtor ledgers, billing volumes and tariff-to-cost benchmarks. Audited statements should replace portfolio medians before investment use.

Known workbook ambiguities

The live factor formula produces 49.0 for the workbook example, while a separate static cell reports 48.5. Several bands also meet at the same boundary, the staff table omits 5 staff/1,000 connections, and liquidity is scored with EBITDA-style percentage bands despite being defined as a ratio. The demo uses the reproducible 49.0 result; the Philippines ranking scores liquidity with current-ratio bands instead and documents deterministic boundary rules in the source code.
