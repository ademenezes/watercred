A relative, renormalized indication — not the full 23-factor index and not a credit rating. Each utility is scored on whichever of the 23 workbook factors its validated data supports, using the workbook's 0–4 bands; weights are renormalized to the available factors (score = Σ points × weight ÷ Σ 4 × weight × 100). At least four evidenced factors are required to rank. Observations more than five years old are excluded, and derived ratios (EBITDA margin, debt to equity, staff per 1,000 connections) only combine same-year statements. Liquidity uses current-ratio bands and negative equity scores 0 on leverage — both demo conventions. Rank shown is the national position; filters narrow the list without re-ranking.

Methodology

How the index and ranking are built.

Each factor receives 0–4 points from the workbook's threshold table. Points are multiplied by fixed weights totaling 100. The full index is the weighted total divided by the 400-point maximum, times 100.

Index=Σ (factor score × weight)÷400 × 100

The ranking applies the same bands and weights but renormalizes over only the factors a utility's audited data supports, so utilities with different evidence remain comparable. The labels below are demo conventions — the workbook defines no rating bands or lending cut-offs.

75–100Indicatively creditworthy
60–74Potentially creditworthy
40–59Below threshold
0–39High concern

Utility size — official LWUA categories

Size uses the Local Water District categories from the DBM/LWUA Revised Manual on Categorization (2011), by number of active service connections:

Very large · A≥ 30,000 connections
Large · B10,000–29,999
Medium · C3,000–9,999
Small · D< 3,000

We classify each utility from the median of its reported connection counts (COA/LWUA), which dampens single-year reporting spikes. Only utilities that report connections can be sized; the rest are shown as size unknown.

Provisional rule: 60 is the minimum score indicating potential creditworthiness, and at least 70% of model inputs (17 of 23) should be evidenced before the result is treated as a reliable indication. This is not an agency letter rating.

The 23 indicators

Definitions, formulas, weights and 0–4 score bands from the source workbook. The nine factors tagged ranked are those the Philippine audited dataset can currently evidence, so only these drive the ranking above.

How is the ranking computed?

Up to nine of the 23 workbook factors can be evidenced per utility: O&M coverage, collection efficiency, non-revenue water, energy and staff cost shares, current liquidity, EBITDA margin (reported, or derived from net income, depreciation, interest and revenue), debt to equity (derived from total liabilities and equity), and staff per 1,000 connections. Each is scored with the workbook's 0–4 bands and weights are renormalized to the factors available. Utilities with fewer than four evidenced factors are listed as unranked. Each row expands to show the exact factors used — value, 0–4 band, weight, data year and source institution.

What data is behind this?

The evidence base is data/philippines_validated.csv: audited financial and operational observations for 519 water utilities, sourced from COA Annual Audit Reports, COA Annual Financial Reports (water districts), LWUA monitoring data and Manila Water statements. Poverty context uses PSA's 2023 Full Year Official Poverty Statistics (15.5%). Amounts reported in PHP million and volumes in million m³ are normalized at load.

What needs due diligence?

The remaining model inputs need utility-level evidence: debt schedules and service coverage for DSCR, cash reserves, grant dependency, debtor ledgers, billing volumes and tariff-to-cost benchmarks. Audited statements should replace any portfolio assumptions before investment use. The utility-to-province mapping is compiled and approximate.
